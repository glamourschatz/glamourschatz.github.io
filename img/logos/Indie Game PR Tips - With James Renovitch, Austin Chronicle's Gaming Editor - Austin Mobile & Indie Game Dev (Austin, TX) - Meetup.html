Indie Game PR Tips - With James Renovitch, Austin Chronicle's Gaming Editor

  • Tell a friend
  • Share

  • Tuesday, November 10, 2015

    6:30 PM

  • Capital Factory (Located in the Austin Centre)

    701 Brazos Street, 16th floor, Austin, TX (map)

    30.268612 -97.740479

  • This month, we're thrilled to feature James Renovitch, Social Media and Gaming Editor at the Austin Chronicle. James will offer tips and recommendations on how indie game devs can best communicate with journalists to promote their releases. (Please note: Due to the holidays, this month's event isn't in our usual 4th Tuesday time slot.)

    James Renovitch has worked at the Austin Chronicle for a decade. He began as an intern and has held various job titles over the years, including proofreader, gaming editor, tech editor, social media editor, listings editor, writer, database custodian, compost wrangler, and amateur volleyball player. During that time, he's been a first-hand witness to many huge changes in the game industry, including the rise of indie and mobile games.

    In a typical day, James' inbox is inundated with roughly 40 press releases, and he does his best to read them all. He also attends and covers many game-related events throughout the year, including Fantastic Arcade and SXSW Interactive. His own tastes veer toward little, personal video games.

    For his talk, James will share some tips on how game devs should interact with game journalists. He'll talk about the best and worst ways to get the attention of the press, how to approach a writer or editor (hint: like a human being), and how to spend your marketing budget or get by with your lack of a budget. Austin is incredibly lucky to have a game writer as friendly and enthusiastic as James. Don't miss what promises to be an immensely helpful event for anyone looking to spread the word about their work!

We're about:
Game Development · Game Design · Mobile Game Development · Mobile Game Design · Independent Game Development · HTML5 Game Development · Mobile Development · iPhone Game Development · Unity 3D · iPad Game Development · Android Game Development · iOS Development · Android Development
x

Austin is home to several large game studios that make AAA titles for console and PC. These folks do amazing work, but the game industry is changing rapidly. Many games are now built by indie studios with small teams and limited budgets, providing more room for experimentation. These upstarts are targeting new platforms like mobile, HTML5, Steam, microconsoles, VR, wearables, etc.

The goal of this group is to highlight this new world of game dev. Our events focus on the nuts and bolts of building games in a small team or for new platforms. We feature talks by professional and hobbyist developers that are heavy on useful takeaways in areas like engineering, game design, art, production, promoting your title, and more. Our events also provide an opportunity to meet potential collaborators, employees, and partners.

Past presenters include Adam Saltsman (creator of Canabalt), Trent Polack (Creative Director of Team Chaos), Dan Magaha (from GameSalad), Randy Smith and David Kalina (of Tiger Style Games), Jonathan Leistiko (board game designer), Patrick Curry (from Unity Austin), John Watson (Technical Director for The Banner Saga), and Matt Piersall (founder of game audio house GL33k).

We welcome any current or aspiring contributors to the Austin mobile/indie game community, including coders, designers, artists, producers, entrepreneurs, and investors. Join us and help push the Austin game dev scene forward!
